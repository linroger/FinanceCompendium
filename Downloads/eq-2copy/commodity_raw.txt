================================================================================
COMMODITY DERIVATIVES AND ENERGY - WORKING NOTES AND EQUATIONS
================================================================================

Equation 1: Futures Price (Cost of Carry)
-----------------------------------------
F = S_0 * e^((r + u - y) * T)

Variables:
- F = Futures price for delivery at time T
- S_0 = Current spot price
- r = Risk-free interest rate
- u = Storage cost as proportion of spot price per unit time
- y = Convenience yield (benefit from physically holding commodity)
- T = Time to delivery in years

Key concepts:
- Generalization of covered interest parity to commodities
- Contango: F > S_0 when r + u > y
- Backwardation: F < S_0 when y > r + u
- Exponential form reflects continuous compounding

References:
- Hull, J. C. (2018). Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives
- Gorton, G. & Rouwenhorst, K. G. (2006). Liquidity, Term Structure, and Efficiency of Commodity Futures

================================================================================

Equation 2: Futures Price with Storage (Discrete)
-------------------------------------------------
F = (S_0 + U) * e^(rT) - V

Variables:
- U = Present value of total storage costs
- V = Present value of convenience benefits
- Other variables same as above

Key concepts:
- Explicit handling of discrete storage costs
- Cash-and-carry arbitrage bounds
- Physical storage economics

================================================================================

Equation 3: Implicit Convenience Yield
--------------------------------------
y = r + u - (1/T) * ln(F/S_0)

Variables:
- Derived from cost-of-carry model
- All variables must be consistent (annualized, continuous)

Key concepts:
- Implied from market prices
- Leading indicator of supply-demand tightness
- High y indicates backwardation

================================================================================

Equation 4: Calendar Spread Value
---------------------------------
V_spread = F_1 - F_2 = e^(-rT_2) * (S_0 * e^(-y_1*T_1) - S_0 * e^(-y_2*T_2))

Variables:
- F_1 = Near-term futures price (delivery at T_1)
- F_2 = Far-term futures price (delivery at T_2)
- y_1, y_2 = Convenience yields for respective periods

Key concepts:
- Isolates time value component
- Captures term structure dynamics
- Lower margin requirements than outright positions

================================================================================

Equation 5: Futures Forward Spread
----------------------------------
Spread = F_1 - F_2

Variables:
- Simple price difference between two futures
- Can be inter-commodity or intra-commodity

Key concepts:
- Foundation of spread trading
- Filters out common price movements
- Captures relative value opportunities

================================================================================

Equation 6: Spread Option (Kirk's Approximation)
------------------------------------------------
V ≈ C(F_1, K*, σ_spread) where K* = K * e^(-(r + u_2)*T_2)

Variables:
- C = Black's call option pricing function
- K* = Adjusted strike
- σ_spread = Effective spread volatility

Key concepts:
- Correlation adjustment for two-asset option
- σ_spread = sqrt(σ_1² + σ_2² - 2ρσ_1σ_2)
- Efficient approximation for trading applications

================================================================================

Equation 7: Black's Spread Option Model
---------------------------------------
V = e^(-rT) * [F_1 * N(d_1) - F_2 * N(d_2)]

Variables:
- N(d) = Standard normal CDF
- d_1 = [ln(F_1/F_2) + 0.5*σ²T] / [σ√T]
- d_2 = d_1 - σ√T

Key concepts:
- Closed-form solution for exchange options
- Assumes bivariate log-normal distribution
- Foundation for crack spread and spark spread options

================================================================================

Equation 8: Crack Spread (3:2:1)
-------------------------------
Crack = 3*P_gas - 2*P_oil - P_ref

Variables:
- P_gas = Gasoline price
- P_oil = Crude oil price
- P_ref = Refining cost/other products
- 3:2:1 ratio = 3 gal gasoline from 1 bbl crude

Key concepts:
- Most actively traded energy spread
- Refining margin proxy
- Seasonal patterns in crack spread

================================================================================

Equation 9: Spark Spread
-----------------------
Spark = P_power - HHV * P_gas

Variables:
- P_power = Electricity price ($/MWh)
- HHV = Higher heating value (~1,027 BTU/scf)
- P_gas = Natural gas price ($/MMBtu)
- Heat rate conversion: 10,000 BTU/kWh = 1 MMBtu/MWh

Key concepts:
- Gas-fired generation margin
- Determines merit-order dispatch
- Sets marginal electricity price in many markets

================================================================================

Equation 10: Clean Spark Spread
-------------------------------
CSS = P_power - HHV * P_gas - emission_costs

Variables:
- emission_costs = EmissionsRate * CarbonPrice
- EmissionsRate ~ 0.4-0.5 tons CO2/MWh for gas

Key concepts:
- True margin in carbon-constrained markets
- Important for EU ETS, California compliance
- Drives fuel switching decisions

================================================================================

Equation 11: Mean-Reverting Spot Price (Ornstein-Uhlenbeck)
-----------------------------------------------------------
dS_t = a(b - S_t)dt + σdW_t

Variables:
- S_t = Spot price at time t
- a = Mean-reversion speed
- b = Long-run mean price
- σ = Instantaneous volatility
- dW_t = Wiener process

Key concepts:
- Gaussian process with analytical tractability
- Captures reversion to equilibrium
- Foundation for commodity price modeling

================================================================================

Equation 12: Schwartz One-Factor Model
--------------------------------------
d ln S_t = κ(θ - ln S_t)dt + σdW_t

Variables:
- ln S_t = Natural log of spot price
- κ = Mean-reversion speed in log-space
- θ = Long-run mean of log-price
- σ = Volatility of log-returns

Key concepts:
- Log-normal mean-reverting dynamics
- Proportional shocks to price level
- Widely used for commodity derivatives pricing

================================================================================

Equation 13: Schwartz Two-Factor Model
--------------------------------------
dS_t = κ_S(θ_S - ln S_t)dt + σ_SdW_S
dδ_t = κ_δ(θ_δ - δ_t)dt + σ_δdW_δ

Variables:
- S_t = Spot price
- δ_t = Convenience yield or net investment return
- Correlated Wiener processes W_S, W_δ

Key concepts:
- Stochastic convenience yield
- Captures price-yield correlation
- Improved forward curve dynamics

================================================================================

Equation 14: Electricity Spot (Langevin)
----------------------------------------
dS_t = -λ(S_t - μ)dt + σdW_t

Variables:
- S_t = Electricity spot price
- λ = Mean-reversion rate (higher than storable commodities)
- μ = Long-run mean price
- σ = Instantaneous volatility

Key concepts:
- Rapid mean-reversion (no storage)
- Extreme spikes followed by rapid decay
- Requires jump-diffusion extension for spikes

================================================================================

Equation 15: Gas Storage Valuation (Dynamic Programming)
--------------------------------------------------------
V_t(S_t) = max[E_t[e^(-rΔ)V_{t+Δ}(S_{t+Δ})], S_t - C_t]

Variables:
- V_t = Value of storage at time t with inventory S_t
- E_t = Conditional expectation
- C_t = Cost of holding inventory

Key concepts:
- Real options approach
- Injection/withdrawal decisions
- Optimal trigger structure for trading

================================================================================

Equation 16: Stack-and-Roll Strategy
------------------------------------
Roll = Σ ΔF_i

Variables:
- ΔF_i = Change in futures price from roll i-1 to roll i
- Sum over all rolls in holding period

Key concepts:
- Continuous futures exposure
- Roll yield = difference between futures return and spot return
- Negative in contango, positive in backwardation

================================================================================

Equation 17: Contango/Backwardation Measure (Basis)
---------------------------------------------------
Basis = F - S = (r + u - y)ST

Variables:
- Linear approximation for small T
- Positive basis = contango
- Negative basis = backwardation

Key concepts:
- Direct measure of market structure
- Cash-and-carry arbitrage bounds
- Storage arbitrage profitability

================================================================================

Equation 18: Calendar Option
---------------------------
V = e^(-rT)E[(F_2 - F_1)+]

Variables:
- Option to establish spread at expiration
- Payoff based on spread at T
- Compound option structure

Key concepts:
- Right to enter spread position
- Protects against unfavorable spread widening
- Useful for timing uncertainty hedging

WEATHER DERIVATIVES
-------------------

- Heating Degree Days (HDD) = max(0, 65 - T_avg) [Fahrenheit]
- Cooling Degree Days (CDD) = max(0, T_avg - 65) [Fahrenheit]
- Cumulative over monthly/seasonal periods
- Payout = max(0, Index - Strike) × Notional

Markets:
- CME weather futures and options (HDD, CDD, precipitation)
- OTC customized structures for specific exposures
- Temperature, rainfall, hurricane indices

Pricing approach:
- Historical burn analysis
- Weather simulation models
- Actuarial methods
- Temperature forecasting models

COMMODITY INDEX ROLL YIELD
--------------------------
Components of commodity index return:
1. Collateral return (risk-free rate on margin)
2. Spot return (change in spot price)
3. Roll return (cash-and-carry over time)

Roll return drivers:
- Forward curve shape (contango vs. backwardation)
- Roll strategy (monthly, quarterly, optimized)
- Contract liquidity and bid-ask spreads
- Index methodology (weighting scheme)

Optimization approaches:
- Threshold rolling (roll only when contango exceeds cost)
- Calendar rolling (spread-based roll timing)
- Multi-contract rolling (smooth transition)
- Dynamic weight optimization

MULTI-COMMODITY OPTIMIZATION
----------------------------
Mean-variance optimization framework:
- Maximize: μ'x - (1/2)λx'Σx
- Subject to: Ax ≤ b, Cx = d

Key considerations for commodities:
- Roll yield as return component
- Basis risk and correlation structure
- Storage constraints and costs
- Liquidity and position limits
- Sector allocation constraints

Risk measures:
- Variance and standard deviation
- Value at Risk (VaR)
- Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR)
- Tracking error relative to benchmark
- Maximum drawdown

================================================================================

JUMP-DIFFUSION MODEL FOR COMMODITY PRICES
-----------------------------------------
dS_t/S_t = (r - y - λμ)dt + σdW_t + (Y - 1)dN_t

Where:
- N_t = Poisson jump process with intensity λ
- Y = Jump size (log-normal distribution)
- μ = E[Y - 1] = expected jump size adjustment

Key concepts:
- Captures price spikes in energy markets
- Separate dynamics for continuous and jump components
- Compound Poisson process for jump arrival
- Relevant for electricity and natural gas

================================================================================

STOCHASTIC VOLATILITY MODEL
---------------------------
dS_t/S_t = (r - y)dt + sqrt(V_t) dW_S
dV_t = κ(θ - V_t)dt + ξ sqrt(V_t) dW_V
dW_S dW_V = ρ dt

Variables:
- V_t = Stochastic variance process (CIR square root process)
- ξ = Volatility of volatility
- ρ = Correlation between price and variance shocks

Key concepts:
- Captures volatility clustering
- Leverages effect (negative correlation)
- Used for commodities with changing volatility regimes
- Forms basis for Heston model extension

================================================================================

FORWARD CURVE MODELING
----------------------
F(t, T) = S_t * exp((r + u - y)(T - t)) + ε

Or multi-factor extension:
F(t, T) = S_t * exp(Σ_i [θ_i(T) - θ_i(t)] * e^(-κ_i(T-t)))

Where:
- θ_i = Factor loadings for each mean-reverting component
- κ_i = Decay rates for each factor
- Multiple factors capture different curve shapes

Key concepts:
- Principal component analysis identifies curve factors
- Level, slope, curvature components
- Applications in curve trading and spread options

================================================================================

Crude Oil to Products Relationships:
- Crack spread = 3*Gasoline + 2*Heating Oil - 4*Crude (simplified)
- 3-2-1 crack spread is standard benchmark
- Spark spread relationship to power prices
- Residual fuel oil spread vs. gas/diesel

Natural Gas to Power Relationships:
- Spark spread = Power - Heat_Rate * Gas
- Dark spread (coal to power) for coal-fired generation
- Clean dark spread includes carbon costs
- Heat rate options and gas price sensitivity

Carbon to Energy Relationships:
- Carbon cost per MWh = Emissions_rate * Carbon_price
- Coal-to-gas switching threshold = Carbon_price / (Emissions_diff)
