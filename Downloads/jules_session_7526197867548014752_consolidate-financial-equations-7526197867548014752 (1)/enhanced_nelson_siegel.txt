**Mathematical Derivation or Foundation**
The Nelson-Siegel model provides a parametric representation of the yield curve using three factors: level (β₀), slope (β₁), and curvature (β₂). The functional form y(τ) = β₀ + β₁(1-e^{-τ/λ})/(τ/λ) + β₂[(1-e^{-τ/λ})/(τ/λ) - e^{-τ/λ)] ensures asymptotic behavior: long-term yields approach β₀, short-term yields equal β₀ + β₁, and the curvature parameter λ controls the speed of convergence. This exponential decay structure captures typical yield curve shapes while maintaining parsimony. The model comes from factor analysis of historical yield curves.

**Key Assumptions and Limitations**
Assumes three-factor structure adequately captures yield curve dynamics. Requires constant decay parameter λ across time. Ignores higher-order factors like volatility smile. Parametric form may not fit extreme market conditions. Assumes arbitrage-free structure. Breaks down during financial crises with unusual curve shapes. Parameter stability may not hold across regimes.
