**Mathematical Derivation or Foundation**
Yield to maturity solves for the internal rate of return that equates present value of cash flows to market price. The equation P_mkt = Î£_{i=1}^n C e^{-y t_i} + F e^{-y t_n} requires numerical solution since y appears in the exponent. This comes from the fundamental principle that bond value equals discounted cash flows. The continuous compounding convention (e^{-y t_i}) differs from semi-annual convention in practice. The solution requires iterative methods due to the transcendental nature of the equation.

**Key Assumptions and Limitations**
Assumes all coupons reinvested at calculated YTM rate. Requires deterministic cash flows with no defaults. Ignores transaction costs and market frictions. Assumes flat yield curve for single YTM. Breaks down for bonds with embedded options. Fails to capture term structure effects. Not applicable to floating rate securities. Reinvestment assumption rarely holds in practice.
