# Company and Projects' Costs of Capital

- Company Cost of Capital (or Weighted Average Cost of Capital – WACC) is the expected return on all the company's outstanding debt and equity  
- Project Cost of Capital is a function of the relative riskiness of the project  
- Market value of firm must be the sum of all its projects

○ If a firm has two assets (projects), A and B, its value must equal

$$
F i r m V a l u e = P V (A B) = P V (A) + P V (B)
$$

# Company and Projects Costs of Capital

- Each asset is valued using its appropriate discount rate

○ "Sum-of-parts" analysis for large multi-line of business companies  

- Discount each project (or asset) at rate at which the market would project it could be purchased independently
- There is no exact science to this

○ Do not focus on getting numbers exactly right  

- Understand key risks and ability to mitigate

# Double Entry Accounting $^{1}$

One entry records what is taken in (revenue) or owned (assets), known as a debit

- A second, offsetting entry records what is given out (e.g. wheat) or owed (e.g. debt), known as a credit  
- Nobel laureate in economics, Thomas Shelling, called double entry bookkeeping "the greatest advance in business management"2

# Balance Sheets and WACC

- We will discuss both accounting based measures and market based measures later  
- WACC for company above equals:

$$
\begin{array}{l} W A C C (w i t h o u t t a x e s) = r _ {D} \times \frac {D}{V} + r _ {E} \times \frac {E}{V} \\ = 7.5 \% \times \frac {30}{100} + 15 \% \times \frac {70}{100} = 12.75 \% \\ \end{array}
$$

- Will explain why the WACC does not change regardless of capital structure when present Modigliani-Miller theorems

# Weighted Average Cost of Capital and Taxes

- WACC for company above equals:

$$
\begin{array}{l} W A C C (w i t h t a x e s) = (1 - T _ {C}) r _ {D} \times \frac {D}{V} + r _ {E} \times \frac {E}{V} \\ = (1 - .21) \times 7.5 \% \times \frac {30}{100} + 15 \% \times \frac {70}{100} = 12.30 \% \\ \end{array}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{l} \beta_ {r e v e n u e} = \beta_ {f i x e d c o s t} \left(\frac {P V (f i x e d c o s t)}{P V (r e v e n u e)}\right) + \\ \beta_ {v a r i a b l e c o s t} \left(\frac {P V (v a r i a b l e c o s t)}{P V (r e v e n u e)}\right) + \beta_ {a s s e t s} \left(\frac {P V (a s s e t)}{P V (r e v e n u e)}\right) \\ \end{array}
