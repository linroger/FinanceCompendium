# Planning Process

- Capital and Operating Budgets

$\circ$  Set in context

Strategic Plan  
Annual Operating Plan

- Corporate and Line of Business
- Goals and Objectives  
Strategy and Tactics

# Planning Process

- Annual plans set goals for each division  
- Typically done "bottom up" with "top down" guidance

Each department provides input  
○ Company management sets rules

- Projects are proposed within this process

○ Must "fit" within company's budget

# Planning Process

Examples:

$\circ$  Total expense may not increase more than five percent  
$\circ$  Grow revenues in battery division by  $12 \%$

- Iterative process  
- Generally reviewed quarterly

# Sensitivity and Scenario Analysis

- All businesses and projects are analyzed under multiple scenarios  
- Text Example: Otobai Company electric scooter

$\circ$  Given assumptions:

20% cost of capital  
Initial investment: ¥15 billion; salvage value ¥0  
Sales increase through year 6 then decline – increase in working capital followed by decrease  
COGS: 50% of sales  

- Taxes:  $40\%$  taxable income (straight-line depreciation)

# Assumptions

Numerous implicit assumptions

○ No legal or regulatory issues (intellectual property)  
○ Minimal liability issues  
○ Additional investment requirements (charging stations)  
$\bigcirc$  Competitive threats

- Margin compression – mitigate through supplier prices  
- Rate of unit or price declines

Market acceptance

# Sensitivity Analysis

- Only certainty: initial forecast will be wrong

Knightian Uncertainty  
$\bigcirc$  Analyze other potential outcomes; create action plans

- Sensitivity to changes in key variables

○ World finds new ways to confuse you

- Misses in volumes and pricing become largest issues  
- Understand volatility versus regime change
