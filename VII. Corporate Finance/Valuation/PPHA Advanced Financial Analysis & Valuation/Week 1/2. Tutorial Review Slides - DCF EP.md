### We use ROIC rather than the more traditional ROA

- ROIC captures only operating items whereas ROA commingles operating and non-operating
- E.g., net income (ROA's numerator) includes both sales revenue (operating) and gains on trading securities (non-operating)  
- E.g., total assets (ROA's denominator) includes both inventory (operating) and excess cash and marketable securities (non-operating)

#### Valuation basics: Dividend discount model ("DDM")

- In theory, the dividend discount model is what underpins value:

![](https://cdn-mineru.openxlab.org.cn/result/2025-11-29/47453518-6200-4f46-8e27-d927912bb10c/57169924d0699baa8b5ac30a2407a633f3ec2f8014baa1eb872dba9b605e1045.jpg)

$$
\mathrm {E q u i t y v a l u e} _ {0} = \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {\infty} \frac {D _ {t}}{\left(1 + r _ {\mathrm {e}}\right) ^ {t}}
$$

- However, the DDM's usefulness relies heavily on the ability to forecast future dividends  
- Dividends are chosen by management and many companies do not pay dividends  
- In practice, we rarely use the DDM

#### Overview of common valuation methods

- Instead, we use measures of flows between the firm and the market(s), e.g., free cash flow, because these flows can be better estimated than dividends

#### Enterprise vs. equity method valuation

- Enterprise valuation models value the flows (cash flows, earnings, etc.) due to all investors (i.e., both equity and debt holders)  
- Equity valuation models value the flows due to equity holders only  
- The two models can be bridged by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} Enterprise value = equity value \\ + debt and debt equivalents \\ - \text{cash and cash equivalents} \\ \end{array}
$$

#### Enterprise DCF review

- This model discounts free cash flow (i.e., cash flow generated by business operations - less any reinvestment back into the business - that is available to all investors) at the weighted average cost of capital (i.e., a blend of the cost of debt and the cost of equity)
- Ideally used to value companies that will maintain their capital structure at a target leverage ratio

##### Four part process

1 Free cash flow ("FCF")  

- Forecast operating line items and line items affecting invested capital  
Calculate net operating profit after tax ("NOPAT") and changes to invested capital  
Calculate projected FCF

2 Discounting

Calculate WACC  
Discount FCFs in forecasted range by WACC

3 Continuing value

Calculate continuing value with long-term growth rate, WACC, and return on new invested capital (RONIC)

Discount continuing value to arrive at present value  
4 Equity value  
Sum present value of FCFs and continuing value to arrive at enterprise value  

- Calculate equity value from enterprise value, net debt, non-controlling interest (if any), preferred stock (if any)

##### Enterprise DCF review (cont.)

###### 1 Free cash flow ("FCF")

- At a high level, free cash flow is calculated as:
- FCF = NOPAT + D&A (depreciation & amortization) – increase in non-cash WC (working capital) - capital expenditures + other adjustments to invested capital

###### 3 Continuing value

Continuing value represents the value of the firm's FCFs after the explicit forecast period

It is typically a perpetuity

- With growth & reinvestment, continuing value is defined as:

$$
\text{Continuing Value}_{t} = \frac{\mathrm{NOPLAT}_{t + 1} \left(1 - \frac{g}{\mathrm{RONIC}}\right)}{\mathrm{WACC} - g}
$$

where:

- $g$  represents the long-term growth rate  
- RONIC represents the return on new invested capital  
Further discounted by WACC to determine present value of continuing value

###### Economic profit

Economic profit measures the value created by the firm in a single period and is defined by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \text{Economic Profit} = \text{Invested Capital} \times (\text{ROIC} - \text{WACC}) \\ \text{Economic Profit} = \text{NOPLAT} - (\text{Invested Capital} \times \text{WACC}) \end{array}
$$

##### Economic profit model review (cont.)

###### 2 Discounting

- Calculate the present value of economic profit in the explicit forecast period by discounting the forecasted figures with WACC:

$$
\mathrm{WACC} = \frac{D}{D + E} k_{d} \left(1 - T_{m}\right) + \frac{E}{D + E} k_{e}
$$

where:

- debt $(D)$ and equity $(E)$ are measured using market values where possible
- $k_{d}$ represents the cost of debt
- $k_{e}$ represents the cost of equity
- $T_{m}$ represents the marginal tax rate, which is used to value the interest tax shield
- Apply a mid-year adjustment to the PVs as appropriate

###### 3 Continuing value

Continuing value represents the value of the firm's economic profit after the explicit forecast period  
Continuing value equals:

$$
\left[ \frac {I C _ {T} x (R O N I C - W A C C)}{(W A C C - g)} \right]
$$

where:

- $IC$  represents invested capital at the end of the forecast period  
- $g$  represents the long-term growth rate  
- RONIC represents the return on new invested capital (and is equal to ROIC in  $\mathrm{T} + 1$ )*
- Further discounted by WACC to arrive at present value of continuing value

If  $\mathrm{RONIC}_{\mathrm{T} + 1}$  does not equal historical ROIC in  $\mathrm{T} + 1$ , then use:

$$
\left[ \frac {E P _ {T + 1}}{W A C C} + \frac {N O P A T _ {T + 1} \left(\frac {g}{R O N I C}\right) (R O N I C - W A C C)}{W A C C (W A C C - g)} \right]
$$

##### Economic profit model review (cont.)

###### Equity value calculation

- Sum (i) the present value of future economic profits, (ii) the present value of the continuing value (of economic profit) and (iii) today's invested capital  
- (+) the present value of non-operating assets (e.g., equity investments) if applicable  
- (-) net debt (debt and debt equivalents minus cash and cash equivalents)  
- (-) the value of preferred stock and noncontrolling interest if applicable  
- Illustrative price per share = equity value divided by the number of shares outstanding
